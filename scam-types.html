Types of Scams We Handle

Online fraud takes many forms. Our team has experience working with clients affected by a wide range of financial scams. Below are the most common types we encounter.

📈

Forex Trading Scams

Unlicensed brokers and fraudulent trading platforms that manipulate results, block withdrawals, or disappear with deposited funds. These operations often use high-pressure tactics and fake profit dashboards to keep victims investing.

Request case review →
₿

Cryptocurrency Scams

Fraudulent crypto exchanges, fake token launches, Ponzi-style investment pools, and wallet phishing schemes. These scams exploit the complexity of blockchain technology to confuse victims and obscure fund movements.

Request case review →
💔

Romance Scams

Scammers build emotional relationships through dating apps or social media, then fabricate emergencies or investment opportunities to extract money over time. Victims often lose significant amounts before recognizing the pattern.

Request case review →
📊

Binary Options Fraud

Unregulated platforms offering all-or-nothing trading contracts with manipulated outcomes. These services often operate without proper licensing and refuse to process withdrawal requests once funds are deposited.

Request case review →
🔒

Phishing & Identity Theft

Sophisticated email, SMS, or website spoofing attacks designed to steal login credentials and personal information. Once accessed, scammers drain bank accounts, open credit lines, or make unauthorized transactions.

Request case review →
💼

Employment & Job Scams

Fake job postings that require upfront payments for training, equipment, or background checks. Some schemes involve victims unknowingly processing fraudulent transactions as part of money laundering operations.
